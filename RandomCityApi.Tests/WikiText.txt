class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Eastchester_(town),_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: History\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The town that is now called Eastchester began settlement in 1664 when ten families migrated from <a href=\"/wiki/Fairfield,_Connecticut\" title=\"Fairfield, Connecticut\">Fairfield, Connecticut</a>. <a href=\"/wiki/Thomas_Pell\" title=\"Thomas Pell\">Thomas Pell</a>, who at that time also owned the territory that is now <a href=\"/wiki/New_Rochelle,_New_York\" title=\"New Rochelle, New York\">New Rochelle</a> and <a href=\"/wiki/Pelham,_New_York\" title=\"Pelham, New York\">Pelham</a>, granted a deed to the group to \"settle down at Hutchinsons',\" where the home of <a href=\"/wiki/Anne_Hutchinson\" title=\"Anne Hutchinson\">Anne Hutchinson</a> had stood some twenty years before. The ten original families were shortly joined by another 26.<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Laws for the region were established the following year, in 1665, under an agreement called the \"Eastchester Covenant\". The covenant was a rare document for this period. It contained 26 provisions, including such items as education of children, disposition and upkeep of property, and support of a minister.\n</p><p>Confirmation of their 1664 <a href=\"/wiki/Land_patent\" title=\"Land patent\">land patent</a> was granted by Governor <a href=\"/wiki/Richard_Nicolls\" title=\"Richard Nicolls\">Richard Nicolls</a> in 1666 after the occupation of the area by the English.<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> A controversy arose in 1700 when the settlers signed a deed with the <a href=\"/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States\" title=\"Native Americans in the United States\">Native Americans</a>. The tract of land involved was known as \"Long Reach\" because of its odd geographical makeup. The sites included are the present <a href=\"/wiki/Bronxville,_New_York\" title=\"Bronxville, New York\">Bronxville</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/Tuckahoe,_Westchester_County,_New_York\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Tuckahoe, Westchester County, New York\">Tuckahoe</a>, and a section of northwest <a href=\"/wiki/Mount_Vernon,_New_York\" title=\"Mount Vernon, New York\">Mount Vernon</a>. The dispute over the ownership of the land involved the towns of New Rochelle, Westchester and the Pell family. When a decision was reached in favor of Eastchester, England's <a href=\"/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain\" title=\"Anne, Queen of Great Britain\">Queen Anne</a> granted a second patent in 1708.<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Eastchester was a farming community at the outbreak of the <a href=\"/wiki/American_Revolution\" title=\"American Revolution\">American Revolution</a>. Although no major battles were fought here, as the heart of the Neutral Ground it saw constant fighting for over 13 years, being harassed by both sides as well as by the cowboys and skinners (the <a href=\"/wiki/Guerrilla\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Guerrilla\">guerrillas</a> of the day). Eastchester's rural makeup began to change with the coming of the railroad in the 1840s. An area of 370 acres (1,500,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of land was incorporated as the village of Mount Vernon in 1853 by a group of New York businessmen;<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the village of Bronxville was incorporated in 1898;<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and the village of Tuckahoe in 1903.<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">&#91;<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)\">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Today, Eastchester is bounded by <a href=\"/wiki/Scarsdale,_New_York\" title=\"Scarsdale, New York\">Scarsdale</a> on the north, New Rochelle on the east, <a href=\"/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York\" title=\"Yonkers, New York\">Yonkers</a> on the west, and Mount Vernon on the south. The town covers approximately five square miles, including Bronxville and Tuckahoe.\n</p><p><a href=\"/wiki/Babe_Ruth\" title=\"Babe Ruth\">Babe Ruth</a> was a member of Leewood Country Club.\n</p><p>The <a href=\"/wiki/Marble_Schoolhouse\" title=\"Marble Schoolhouse\">Marble Schoolhouse</a> and <a href=\"/wiki/Tuckahoe_High_School\" title=\"Tuckahoe High School\">Tuckahoe High School</a> are listed on the <a href=\"/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places\" title=\"National Register of Historic Places\">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-nris_6-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-nris-6\">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1899, the University system of New York State granted a charter to the Waverly Union School, located in present day Eastchester (called Upper Tuckahoe at the time), which housed grades kindergarten through twelve. This charter marks the beginnings of the Eastchester Union Free District. Waverly was the center of the Eastchester educational community, until Eastchester High School was completed in 1927. Waverly continued to serve as an elementary school until 1983. In 1990, it was refurbished and opened as the Waverly Early Childhood Center.\n</p><p>The Wilmot School near the corner of White Plains Road and Wilmot Road was a K-8 school that serviced the north end of town after 1909. When the Greenvale elementary school was erected in 1936, the Wilmot School was converted into Eastchester's Town Hall. In 1964, the clubhouse of the Oak Ridge Country Club was converted into the present Town Hall, and the Wilmot School was torn down. The Cooper School, located in the northern end of town, was an elementary school built in the 1920s. It was demolished in 1980 and the land sold to a private housing developer. The original schoolyard lives on as Cooper Field, leased to the town of Eastchester at the rate of one dollar per year. The park is also referred to as Vaccaro Park, as it was originally donated by Carmela Vaccaro<sup id=\"cite_ref-7\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-7\">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the matriarch of one of the largest and longest standing families in Eastchester. The Anne Hutchinson School, another elementary school, was built in 1953, and additional classrooms constructed several years later. A primary wing was built at the Greenvale School in the 1950s.<sup id=\"cite_ref-8\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-8\">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" title=\"Census\">census</a><sup id=\"cite_ref-GR2_10-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-GR2-10\">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> of 2000<sup class=\"plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update\" style=\"display:none;\"><a class=\"external text\" href=\"//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eastchester_(town),_New_York&amp;action=edit\">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, there were 31,318 people, 12,626 households, and 8,408 families residing in the town.  The <a href=\"/wiki/Population_density\" title=\"Population density\">population density</a> was 6,376.3 people per square mile (2,462.7/km²).  There were 13,035 housing units at an average density of 2,653.9 per square mile (1,025.0/km²).  The racial makeup of the town was 87.35% <a href=\"/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"White (U.S. Census)\">White</a>, 5.80% <a href=\"/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"African American (U.S. Census)\">Black</a> or <a href=\"/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Race (United States Census)\">African American</a>, 0.07% <a href=\"/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Native American (U.S. Census)\">Native American</a>, 3.85% <a href=\"/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Asian (U.S. Census)\">Asian</a>, 0.03% <a href=\"/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)\">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.40% from <a href=\"/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Race (United States Census)\">other races</a>, and 1.51% from two or more races. <a href=\"/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Hispanic (U.S. Census)\">Hispanic</a> or <a href=\"/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Latino (U.S. Census)\">Latino</a> of any race were 5.48% of the population.\n</p><p>There were 12,626 households out of which 31.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 55.7% were <a href=\"/wiki/Marriage\" title=\"Marriage\">married couples</a> living together, 8.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.4% were non-families. 29.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 13.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.46 and the average family size was 3.09.\n</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 23.8% under the age of 18, 5.4% from 18 to 24, 29.8% from 25 to 44, 24.2% from 45 to 64, and 16.7% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 40 years. For every 100 females, there were 87.7 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 83.0 males.\n</p><p>According to a 2007 estimate, the median income for a household in the town was $101,425, and the median income for a family was $137,663. Males had a median income of $71,420 versus $47,725 for females. The <a href=\"/wiki/Per_capita_income\" title=\"Per capita income\">per capita income</a> for the town was $49,941.  About 3.0% of families and 4.2% of the population were below the <a href=\"/wiki/Poverty_line\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Poverty line\">poverty line</a>, including 3.4% of those under age 18 and 5.2% of those age 65 or over.\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Arts_and_culture\">Arts and culture</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a
